Executive Summary


In this analysis, I visualize three years of a beverage company performance data (FY2014-2016) across three regions and multiple product categories. SAP Analytics Cloud was used to build this visualisation. The model used to develop this visualisation follwed Star Schema, with Date, Product and Location granualrities developed from it. The goal was to identify margin optimization opportunities and provide actionable recommendations to decesion-makers.

Key Findings

1. Regional Performance Disparities

- California shows significantly higher gross margins 34-46% compared to Nevada and Oregon, particularly in the Alcohol category
- Nevada consistently underperforms with margins 15-31% below California across all product categories

Recommendation:

- Implement California's best practices in inventory management and pricing strategies across Nevada and Oregon operations
- Conduct detailed cost structure analysis in Nevada to identify efficiency gaps
Target: Potential 10-15% margin improvement in Nevada operations within 12 months

2. Product Portfolio Performance

- Carbonated drinks maintains the highest gross margin contribution (42.3-42.7%)with consistent stability
- Alcohol category shows steady growth in margins (20.8% to 23.7%)
- Juices brings in the highest revenue, contributing 62.7% across 3 FY

Recommendation:

- Increase allocation of marketing and distribution resources to carbonated drinks in Nevada and Oregon
- Leverage the market share in the juices market by taking efforts to withold the competitive advantage
Target: 5% increase in high-margin product sales mix within 2 quarters

3. Seasonality Trends

- H1 consistently shows higher margins across all product categories
- Juices has shown a significant and steady Revenue growth in the last 3 halves
- Declining revenue trends and mediocre gross margin % in the alcohol product line, consistenly being the weakest performer in the last few years.

Recommendation:

- Increase allocation of marketing and distribution resources to carbonated drinks in Nevada and Oregon
- Optimize inventory levels based on H1/H2 performance patterns
- Create CBA (Cost benefit Analysis) for Alcohol Product line and simultaneously using ML forecasting to conclude if alcohol is profitable and if so, where.
Target: 5% increase in high-margin product sales mix within 2 quarters

Success Metrics:

- Monthly margin tracking by region and product
- Inventory turnover rates
- Product mix ratios
- Regional performance variance.
